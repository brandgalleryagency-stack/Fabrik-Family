Five Panels From Zawadi's World

Rift Valley, Kenya  ·  A cotton harvest  ·  A blue notebook  ·  The full length of the world

01 · The Dream

At night, when the house
is quiet, she writes.

Zawadi is nineteen. At night, when the house is quiet, she opens a small blue notebook and writes numbers by the light of a lamp.

On one page she draws something again and again — the shop she hopes to open one day. A door. A window. Cloth folded in careful stacks.

02 · The Future

In her mind
the shop is already real.

Fabric in the window. Light on the floor. A dress on the doorframe.

She knows exactly where the cloth will come from. She will grow it herself. She will pick it with her own hands. And she will be able to tell every customer exactly where it started.

03 · The Hands That Began It

The hands that taught her
belong to her mother.

For years those hands have moved through the cotton rows, opening the white bolls one by one. Soft fibre gathered gently, so it can travel.

Cotton does not stay cotton for long. Soon it will become thread. Then fabric. Then something someone will wear.

04 · The Beginning of a Garment

Now Zawadi walks
the same rows.

The cotton is soft and bright in her hands. She does not know where it will go.

Only that somewhere far from this field, someone will one day cut it into fabric.

05 · The Hidden History

Their hands will
still be there.

Somewhere far away, someone will one day wear a garment made from this cotton — and it will feel right.

She will not know Zawadi. She will not know Amina. But their hands will still be there.

She does not know that this feeling
has a history.
